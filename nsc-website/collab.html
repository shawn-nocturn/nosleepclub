Collective & Promoter Partnerships

We build with people who care about the room.

No Sleep Club is a Toronto-based electronic music collective — minimal house and tech house, all underground. We're looking for collectives, promoters, and crews who share our standards. If you care about the music and the long game — we should talk.

Our vision
We think in decades, not quarters.

No Sleep Club started as four DJs in Toronto who were tired of seeing the same thing — events that prioritized numbers over experience, promoters who disappeared after the last track, scenes that burned hot and died because nobody was building with intention.

So we built something different. Every sound check, every lineup, every room — curated with the same care whether it's 150 people or 1,000. We don't chase trends. We don't book for clout. We book because the music is right and the energy is real.

One year in: 20+ events produced. 10+ venue partnerships. 2 festivals. 23+ roster artists. Collectives like Dopamine Machine, Premiere Music Group, and Overlord trusted us enough to build together. Not because we moved fast — because we moved with purpose.

Every decision gets filtered through one question: would we be proud of this in ten years?

We're looking for collaborators who think the same way. Not people who want to slap two logos on a flyer and call it a collab — people who want to build something together that neither of us could do alone.

Ways to work together
No rigid playbook. Here's what's worked.

The best collabs come from real conversation. These formats have worked for us and for partners like Dopamine Machine, Premiere Music Group, and Overlord.

Co-Headline

Joint Event

Both crews bring their community, split the lineup, share the room. Two audiences meet — everyone walks away with new people to follow. This is where scenes grow.

Showcase

Artist Exchange

You feature our artists at your event, we feature yours at ours. Cross-pollinate audiences without the overhead of co-producing a full show. Low lift, high impact.

Series

Recurring Partnership

Not a one-off — a recurring series built together over time. Monthly, bi-monthly, seasonal. The best partnerships aren't transactional. They're relational.
